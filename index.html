NeuBase: Spline Surfaces with Neural Basis Functions

Neural parametric surfaces that fit accurately and deform in real time

Anshul Mendiratta Lei Yang Xin Li John Keyser Scott Schaefer Wenping Wang

Texas A&M University  ·  The University of Hong Kong

Paper (ACM) Video Code (soon)

Abstract

We introduce NeuBase, a neural parametric surface representation that both accurately fits target surfaces with fine geometric detail and supports intuitive real-time surface deformation. NeuBase consists of a Catmull–Clark subdivision base surface and an offset field defined by a set of neural basis functions encoded via a neural map. By construction, NeuBase surfaces exhibit four fundamental geometric properties — linearity, locality, smoothness, and affine equivariance — enabling real-time, direct manipulation without retraining the neural network. In addition, we propose a scalable neural map that maintains memory efficiency even for complex shapes with dense control meshes. Experiments on a large-scale dataset demonstrate that our method achieves better fitting accuracy than state-of-the-art neural parametric surface representations.

Results

Surface Fitting & Deformation

We evaluate on 75 shapes from the MPZ dataset. NeuBase consistently outperforms NGF and NeuPPS across point-to-surface distance (P2S), Hausdorff distance (HD), and normal angular error (NAE), while using comparable storage — and reduces P2S to roughly ¼ of conventional CC subdivision surfaces.

Method P2S (×10⁻⁴) ↓ HD (×10⁻³) ↓ NAE (°) ↓ Storage (KB) ↓
CC Subdivision ~4× ours >2× ours >2× ours comparable
NGF 8.401 14.16 6.412 ~156
NeuPPS 5.472 13.21 4.641 ~155
NeuBase (Ours) 4.556 12.83 4.251 ~153

Average over 75 MPZ shapes. Lower is better. NeuBase achieves the best accuracy across all metrics at comparable storage.

NeuBase also supports real-time shape deformation at 25–40 fps by directly manipulating control vertices or offset vectors, without any network retraining. Global rigid transformations and scaling are exact; local edits affect only the local neighbourhood, preserving fine geometric detail elsewhere.
